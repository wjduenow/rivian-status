# status-light — the `rivian-status` enclosures

The headless Rivian charge/range light: a **Seeed XIAO ESP32-S3** driving an **8-pixel
WS2812 stick** that shows the pixels out the front. There are **two enclosure versions** —
both drive the same board, and both are generated by the same Python CSG toolchain.

| version | form factor | printed parts | size |
|---|---|---|---|
| **[box/](box/)** (v1) | desk-standing "top-bar" box; pixels face **up** out a lid window | `shell.stl` + `lid.stl` | 75.3 × 34.3 × **16** mm |
| **[box-v2/](box-v2/)** (v2) | **slip case that press-fits over a Nekmit flat USB wall charger**; pixels face **out** the front | `case.stl` + `cover.stl` | protrudes **29.1** mm from the wall |

### v1 — `box/` (desk light)
A two-part screwed box. The XIAO lies flat on the floor; the WS2812 stick is stacked **above**
it on two posts with the LEDs facing **up** through a window in the lid. USB-C exits the back
wall; the U.FL antenna coils inside. See **[box/README.md](box/README.md)**.

### v2 — `box-v2/` (wall-charger case)
A one-piece slip shell that **press-fits over a Nekmit Ultra-Thin flat wall charger** — the
charger is the mechanical *and* electrical anchor (it plugs into the wall; AC prongs exit the
**open back**; an inward snap lip retains it). The WS2812 stick mounts **vertically** on the
front behind the window, screwed from the open back into hidden bosses. The XIAO rests flat on
the lower **skirt** floor, powered by a USB-A→USB-C pigtail off the charger. The skirt's
wall-facing back is a **separate screw-on cover** (so the body prints open-backed, no supports).
See **[box-v2/README.md](box-v2/README.md)** for the full parts list + build. Two printed parts.

## Conventions (shared with `../../../sonos-nest/hardware`)
- Python CSG toolchain (trimesh + manifold3d), the `img23d` conda env — **not** OpenSCAD.
- Every dimension lives in a `*_params.py`; builders `assert` their own clearances.
- Board numbers come from vendor CAD; anything estimated is flagged **⚠️VERIFY** and
  caliper-checked before a final print.
- Keep `hardware/` commits separate from firmware — this is user-owned mechanical work.
